# Mounjaro (tirzepatide) — effect analysis

Personal, **n = 1** look at **clinic weight / BMI** and **documented tirzepatide dose** from Duke **MyChart After Visit Summaries (AVS)** — useful for a coarse “what happened on paper,” not a causal trial.

**Last updated:** 2026-03-20 (figures match AVS snapshots reviewed that day).

---

## What this is (and isn’t)

| In scope | Out of scope |
|----------|----------------|
| Vitals and med-list **START / STOP / CHANGE** lines from AVS **“Today’s Visit”** / medication sections | Exact injection dates, adherence, diet, exercise, or other meds’ effects |
| One primary-care provider’s chart (**Daniel J. Bujnowski, MD**) plus noted Epic entries (e.g. **H. Abate, MD** on **2025-02-19**) | Generalizing to other patients or label-efficacy claims |

**Interpretation:** AVS weight is **clinic scale at visits**; dose rows are **prescription / instruction snapshots**. Alignment between columns is **descriptive**, not proof that a given dose change *caused* a weight change.

## Magnitude (back-of-envelope)

- **Total change (first → last in-person row):** about **427.7 lb → 341.2 lb** → **~86.5 lb** over **~19.5 months** (2024-07-02 → 2026-02-16).
- **BMI (AVS):** about **59.7 → 47.6** over the same span.
- **Average pace:** order of **~0.14 lb/day** or **~1 lb/week** if spread evenly — real life is **not** linear (see below).

These are **descriptive**; they don’t isolate tirzepatide from concurrent changes (e.g. BP meds, diet advice, other conditions).

## Dose milestones vs weight slope (qualitative)

1. **2024-07 → 2024-09:** Rapid early loss on **2.5 mg** (427 lb → ~392 lb by mid-Aug; **2024-07-25** ~409 lb shows progression within weeks). **2024-09-10** AVS: **5 mg** pen — weight continues to drift down on subsequent refills (no in-office weight in that interval on this sheet).

2. **2024-08 → 2025-02:** **2025-02-13** weight (**397 lb**) is **slightly above** **2024-08-16** (**~392 lb**) — consistent with **noise**, fluid, or life factors; not a clean monotonic curve on every visit.

3. **2025-02 → 2025-06:** Dose story moves toward **7.5 mg pen** then **10 mg/week** via pen volume (**2025-06-18**); weight moves **397 → 387 lb** between those office visits.

4. **2025-06 → 2026-02:** On **10 mg/week** then **10 mg pen** (**2025-08-12** telephone AVS), weight moves **387 → ~341 lb** — the largest **single-interval drop** on this table, but the interval is also the **longest** between listed office weights (~8 months).

**Takeaway:** The chart shows **substantial weight loss** overlapping **titration toward 10 mg** and **stable 10 mg** periods; it does **not** prove which interval “worked best,” because visit spacing and adherence aren’t captured here.

## Limitations (read before sharing)

- **AVS ≠ research data:** One row’s weight may be morning vs afternoon, different scales, clothing, etc.
- **Dose timeline ≠ injection log:** “First clear AVS” for a strength may lag what you actually took.
- **Confounding:** Blood pressure medication changes (e.g. **2025-06-18**, **2026-02-16**) and other care aren’t modeled here.
